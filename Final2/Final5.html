In the United States alone there are 250,00 rivers which means to any Fly angler that is a lot of fish. Rivers come in many different shapes and sizes and there are many factors that go into matching a fly to a river such as the location of the river, the depth of the river, and how fast the river is moving. Each lure or fly that you use is supposed to resemble and actually match the fly that is currently hatching or hatched on the fiver, some of these flys float on top of the water which is when you would want to use a drive fly that floats but some flys/bugs sink below the surface which is where you would want to use a nymph or something that sits in a certain depth of the water column. One very popular state for flyfishing is Montana home of blue ribbon fly waters such as the Missouri River.
